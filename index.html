BIRTH

Jesus, also known as Jesus of Nazareth or Jesus Christ, is believed to have been born in Bethlehem around 4 to 6 B.C. According to Christian tradition, his birth marks a significant event in human history and is celebrated on December 25th. The Gospels of Matthew and Luke describe his miraculous conception through the Holy Spirit and his birth to the Virgin Mary. The event is central to the Christian faith, symbolizing God's incarnation in human form.

MISSION

Jesus' mission was characterized by teachings of love, compassion, and redemption. He gathered disciples and preached about the Kingdom of God, urging people to repent and turn to a life of righteousness. His ministry included performing miracles, such as healing the sick and raising the dead, to demonstrate divine authority. His teachings emphasized forgiveness, humility, and selflessness. Jesus' ultimate mission, according to Christian belief, was to provide salvation through his sacrificial death on the cross, serving as atonement for humanity's sins.

DEATH

Jesus' crucifixion is a pivotal event in Christian theology. He was arrested, tried, and sentenced to death by crucifixion. According to the Gospels, he willingly accepted this fate as part of his divine purpose. Christians believe that through his death, Jesus took on the sins of humanity, offering a path to reconciliation with God. Three days later, Christians celebrate the Resurrection, asserting that Jesus rose from the dead, conquering sin and death. This event is foundational to the Christian faith, symbolizing the hope of eternal life for believers.
